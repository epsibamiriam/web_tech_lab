Ocean Life Display


Sea creatures encompass a vast array of animals that live in marine environments. This includes fish like sharks and clownfish, marine mammals like whales and dolphins, various invertebrates like jellyfish, starfish, and octopus, as well as crustaceans like crabs and shrimp.

FISH

Sharks, clownfish, anglerfish, and pufferfish are just a few of the many fish species that inhabit the ocean.

MARINE MAMMALS

Whales (like blue whales and orcas), dolphins, and seals are examples of mammals adapted to marine life. .

INVERTEBRATES

This group includes a wide range of animals, such as jellyfish, starfish, octopus, squid, and various types of worms and crustaceans.

Habitats of Marine animals

Marine habitats for sea animals vary widely and can be broadly categorized into coastal and open ocean environments. Coastal habitats include intertidal zones, estuaries, salt marshes, kelp forests, seagrass meadows, and coral reefs. Open ocean habitats are found beyond the continental shelf and include the surface waters, deep sea, and ocean floor.

About bull kelp
Bull kelp grows and thrives in rough-and-tumble coastal waters. It's aided by its rootlike holdfast, which has many fingerlike projections (haptera) that hold the kelp tight to rocks.

Meet the basket star
A basket star depends on passing currents for food. It extends long arms and uncurls its branches to form an intricate net. Then it gathers a meal of tiny drifting animals, like small crustaceans and plankton.

Meet the beaded comb jelly
This jelly fishes for its meals. Do you see the “beads” strung on this comb jelly’s two dangling tentacles? Those beads are coiled branches that flick out to catch tiny crustaceans. Then the jelly uses its tentacles to reel in its prey.
